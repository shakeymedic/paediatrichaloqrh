How to Use This Guide
1. The Two-Page System
Page A: Walkthrough (Preparation) — Use during the initial "Kit Dump" phase. Contains equipment checklists, team roles, and setup instructions.
Page B: Flowchart (Action) — Use during the procedure. Designed for high-stress cognitive processing with critical steps highlighted in red.
2. Design Principles
Standard Steps (Grey):  Routine actions and setups.
Critical Steps (Red):  Dangerous/irreversible actions — pause and confirm.
Paeds Tip (Green):  Paediatric-specific modifications.
Pro-Tip (Blue):  Technique pearls and cognitive aids.
Plan B (Red Box):  Fallback if primary method fails.
3. Human Factors & Safety
Allocate a Reader:  Hand this booklet to a specific team member to read steps aloud, reducing cognitive load for the operator.
Pre-Procedure Pause:  5-second pause before any incision. Confirm landmarks verbally.
Weight Estimation:  Use Broselow tape if available. Formula: <1yr=(0.5×months)+4, 1-5yr=(2×age)+8, >5yr=(3×age)+7.

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
• Call 2222 — "Paediatric Obstetric / Cardiac Arrest"
• Activate: EM Reg (lead), Paediatric Reg, Obstetric Reg, Anaesthetics, Neonatal Team (NLS)
• Indication: Cardiac arrest AND uterus ≥ umbilicus (≈≥20/40)
• If no ROSC within 4 minutes of effective CPR → proceed with RH
• Goal: deliver within 5 minutes of arrest

ALS Modifications (begin immediately):
• Manual LUD — push uterus LEFT with both hands
• IV access ×2 above diaphragm (or IO if difficult)
• Early intubation: ETT = (Age÷4)+3.5 (cuffed), assume difficult airway
• Remove fetal monitors
Equipment Checklist
Chlorhexidine (rapid splash)
Sterile Drape / Gown Pack
Scalpel: Size 10 or 20
Tuff Cut Scissors
Suction ×2 (Yankauer + wide bore)
Large Swabs (for packing)
Stapler / Clingfilm / Large Dressings
Umbilical Cord Clamps ×2
Artery Forceps ×2
Scissors or Scalpel (for cord)
Age-appropriate ETT + BVM
Broselow Tape
📍 Storage Location
NEONATAL RESUS KIT IN PAEDIATRIC RESUS BAY
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Prepare: Rapid chlorhexidine splash. Continue CPR throughout.
  2. Skin Incision: Vertical midline — Xiphisternum to Pubic Symphysis. Deep through skin and fat with SCALPEL.
  3. Peritoneum: Bluntly enter with fingers (tear open) — avoids bowel injury.
  4. Expose Uterus: Retract abdominal wall laterally.
  5. Uterine Incision: Vertical in upper segment of uterus. SCALPEL to start, extend with TUFF CUT SCISSORS.
  6. Protect Baby: Insert two fingers under uterine wall, away from fetal head. Extend cut superiorly.
  7. Delivery: Scoop head and shoulders. Do NOT pull by a single limb.
  8. Cord: Clamp ×2. Cut between clamps. Hand to NLS Team immediately.
  9. Placenta: One gentle tug. If stuck — leave in situ. Pack uterus with SWABS.
🩺 Adolescent uterus may be smaller — start with smaller stab incision
Post-Procedure Plan
ROSC: Mass closure (dressings/clingfilm/staples). Emergent Theatre. Continue ALS during transfer.
Drugs: Oxytocin 5 units IV slowly (NEVER rapid bolus — risk of re-arrest), Antibiotics, TXA 15mg/kg IV (max 1g)
Neonate: Dedicated NLS team only. NICU activation ≥22/40.
Vertical midline incision — speed over sterility

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Page 2: Action Flowchart — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

  • GOAL: DELIVERY <5 MINS | Indication: Cardiac arrest. Uterus ≥ umbilicus. No ROSC after 4 mins CPR
  • Call 2222 "Paediatric Obstetric Arrest"
0
IMMEDIATE ALS MODIFICATIONS
  • CPR: Standard ACLS + Manual LUD (push LEFT — do NOT tilt)
  • Airway: ETT = (Age÷4)+3.5 cuffed. Assume difficult airway.
  • IV Access: ×2 above diaphragm. Use IO if peripheral difficult.
Remove fetal monitors — impede resuscitation quality
1
ABDOMINAL INCISION
  • Pause 5 seconds. Confirm landmarks verbally.
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubic Symphysis.
  • Deep through skin and fat. Bluntly separate rectus muscles.
  • Enter peritoneum bluntly with fingers — do not use blade.
Ignore bleeding — no cardiac output during arrest.
2
UTERINE INCISION
  • Vertical incision upper uterine segment (avoids bladder/arteries).
  • Insert two fingers to protect fetal face.
  • Extend with TUFF CUT SCISSORS toward fundus.
🩺 Adolescent uterus may be smaller — use smaller initial stab.
3
DELIVERY
  • Scoop head and shoulders. Do NOT pull on single limb.
  • Rotate to disengage from pelvis if needed.
  • Clamp cord ×2. Cut between clamps. Hand to NLS team.
4
POST-DELIVERY & CLOSURE
  • Placenta: One gentle tug. If stuck — leave in situ. Pack with SWABS.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 5 units IV slowly, Antibiotics, TXA 15mg/kg (max 1g) Fetch Drugs
  • Closure: Pack uterus. Dressings/clingfilm/staples.
  • Transfer: Emergent Theatre if ROSC.
⚠️ Paediatric Note: Oxytocin
Give 5 units IV over ≥1 minute. Never rapid bolus in adolescents — profound hypotension risk. Consider carboprost/ergometrine (obstetric advice).

Emergency Breech Delivery

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

🩺 PAEDIATRIC EDITION
Initial Actions
• Call 2222 "Obstetric Emergency" + "Neonatal Emergency"
• Position: Lithotomy. Buttocks to edge of bed.
• Team: EM Reg (lead), Paediatric Reg, Obstetric Reg, Anaesthetics, Neonatal Team

GOLDEN RULE — HANDS OFF:
• Do NOT touch baby until umbilicus is visible
• Allow baby to hang — gravity delivers
• Deliver head within 5 minutes of buttock delivery

Paeds Considerations:
• Adolescent pelvis may be narrower — be prepared for head entrapment
• Concealed pregnancy: gestational age may be unknown — prepare NLS for all ≥22/40
• Warm environment critical — switch on Resuscitaire heater immediately
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Sterile Drape
Neonatal Resuscitation Kit
Resuscitaire (Heater on) Switch On Now
Oxytocin — weight-based Fetch Drug
GTN Spray (uterine relaxation) Fetch Drug
Episiotomy Scissors
Cord Clamps ×2
Broselow Tape
Post-Procedure
• Inspect perineum for tears (3rd/4th degree)
• Active management 3rd stage: Oxytocin weight-based IV
• Paediatric review for baby (hips, limbs, cord)
• Transfer to Obstetric Unit / Theatre for repair
Manoeuvres (if descent stops)
  1. Legs Stuck (Pinard's): Insert finger behind fetal knee (popliteal fossa). Abduct thigh laterally. Knee flexes. Sweep out.
  2. Arms Stuck (Lovset's): Hold bony pelvis (thumbs on sacrum). Rotate 180° (keep back up). Posterior arm sweeps down. Deliver arm. Rotate back 180° for second arm.
  3. Head Stuck (Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit): Assistant: Suprapubic pressure. Operator: Baby on forearm. Fingers on malar eminences. Flex head. Pull gently.
  4. Head Trapped — Adolescent Pelvis: GTN Spray 400mcg sublingual (1-2 puffs) — uterine relaxation in ~60 sec. Call Obstetric Reg if not present. Consider Dührssen's cervical incisions (specialist only).
🩺 Adolescent Pelvis: May be android/anthropoid shaped with narrower subpubic angle. Head entrapment is more likely. Have GTN available. Episiotomy alone will NOT resolve head entrapment.
Pinard's: Abduct thigh to flex knee

TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
• Call 2222 "Trauma Cardiac Arrest". Activate Major Haemorrhage Protocol. Request O-neg blood.
• Consider WMAS/HEMS — patient will need transfer to MTC (QE Birmingham).

PAEDIATRIC INDICATIONS (tighter than adult):
• PENETRATING thoracic/epigastric: Arrest <15 mins + signs of life (pupil response, gasping, ECG activity, cardiac motion on US)
• BLUNT trauma: Witnessed loss of cardiac output in ED only
• Signs of Life (SOL) = pupils reacting, gasping, ECG activity, cardiac motion on USS

PAEDIATRIC CONTRAINDICATIONS:
• Pre-hospital CPR >15 mins after penetrating injury without SOL
• Blunt trauma without witnessed arrest in ED
• Severe head injury + cardiac arrest

🩺 Adolescents (16-18): ~5% survival with penetrating + SOL. True paediatric (<15): 0% survival in blunt, rare survivors in penetrating. Age alone should not preclude attempt in penetrating + SOL.

Goals: Release tamponade | Internal cardiac massage | Cross-clamp descending aorta
Equipment Tray
Chlorhexidine
Sterile Drape / Gown Pack
Scalpel: Size 22
Tuff Cut Scissors (Heavy Duty)
Gigli Saw (Handles + Wire) or Bone Cutters
Finochietto Retractor (pliable ribs in young children)
Toothed Forceps (Bonney's)
Vascular Clamp (Satinsky/DeBakey)
Internal Paddles (for Defib)
Artery Forceps (Spencer Wells/Kellys)
Foley Catheter 12-14F (<30kg), 20F (larger)
Prolene/Silk 2-0 (Horizontal Mattress)
Skin Stapler / Large Dressings
📍 Storage
FINOCHIETTO RETRACTOR AND GIGLI SAW IN THORACOTOMY TRAY
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Position: Supine. Left arm extended overhead (exposes left thorax).
  2. Incision: Left anterolateral thoracotomy — 4th or 5th ICS (below nipple, above 5th rib). Incise from sternum to mid-axillary line.
  3. Enter Chest: Tuff Cut scissors through intercostal muscles. Finger to guide entry. Avoid lung.
  4. Retract: Insert Finochietto retractor (or manually retract ribs). In young children, ribs are pliable — may not need saw.
  5. Pericardiotomy: Grasp pericardium anterior to phrenic nerve. Vertical incision — release tamponade. Remove clot manually.
  6. Internal Cardiac Massage: Two-handed compression (or one-handed in small children). 100 compressions/min.
  7. Aortic Cross-Clamp: Identify descending aorta (left of spine). Clamp with Satinsky clamp. Shunts blood to coronary + cerebral.
  8. Defibrillate: VF/pVT — Internal paddles 10-20J.
🩺 Paediatric ribs are more pliable — manual spreading may suffice without Gigli saw in children <10 yrs. One-handed cardiac massage for small children.
Post-Procedure Plan
If ROSC: Close chest with large dressings. Immediate Transfer to Cardiothoracics / MTC. Sedate and paralyse. Maintain aortic clamp during transfer if clamped.
Left anterolateral thoracotomy — 4th/5th ICS

  • PENETRATING + SOL <15 mins = PROCEED | BLUNT = witnessed ED arrest only | BLUNT without SOL = do NOT proceed

1
LEFT ANTEROLATERAL THORACOTOMY
  • Incision: 4th/5th ICS, sternum to mid-axillary line, below nipple/above 5th rib.
  • Divide intercostal muscles with Tuff Cut scissors (blade up, finger guide).
  • Spread ribs — Finochietto OR manual in young/pliable ribs.
In children <10 yrs, ribs may spread manually without Gigli saw.
2
PERICARDIOTOMY & TAMPONADE RELEASE
  • Grasp pericardium anterior to phrenic nerve. Vertical incision.
  • Open longitudinally. Remove clot manually.
  • Internal cardiac massage: 2-handed adults/older teens. 1-handed in small children.
3
CARDIAC MASSAGE & DEFIB
  • Rate: ~100 compressions/min.
  • VF/pVT: Internal paddles — 10-20J (paediatric setting).
4
AORTIC CROSS-CLAMP
  • Identify descending aorta posterior-left of thoracic cavity.
  • Clamp with Satinsky clamp. Diverts blood to cerebral/coronary circulation.
  • Do NOT release clamp until haemorrhage controlled.
5
WOUNDS & CLOSURE
  • Cardiac wounds: Suture or Foley balloon tamponade (12-14F for small children).
  • Lung: Clamp pulmonary hilum if massive haemorrhage.
  • Closure: Dressings/staples. Immediate transfer.
STOP CRITERIA: >30 mins CPR without ROSC | No cardiac activity on direct visualisation | Non-survivable injuries

BURNS EMERGENCY

Escharotomy (Burns)

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
• Call 2222 "Burns Emergency". Early contact with Regional Burns Centre.
• Indication: Full thickness circumferential burns causing:
  – Chest: Respiratory failure (reduced chest expansion, rising pressures)
  – Limb: Vascular compromise (absent distal pulse, CRT >2s, pain/pallor/paraesthesia)

Fluid Resuscitation (start BEFORE escharotomy):
PAEDIATRIC Parkland: 3ml × weight(kg) × %TBSA (full thickness only)
Half in first 8 hours, half over next 16 hours. Use Hartmann's or Plasmalyte.
Urine output target: >1ml/kg/hr in children <30kg
Threshold for resus: >10% TBSA in children (vs 15% adults)
BBA referral threshold: ≥2% TBSA in children (vs ≥3% adults)

🩺 TBSA: Use Lund-Browder chart (NOT Rule of Nines — less accurate in children). Child's palm + fingers ≈ 1% TBSA.
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Sterile Drape / Gown Pack
Scalpel: Size 22 or 10
Surgical Diathermy (preferred — reduces bleeding)
Large Swabs
Dressings (paraffin gauze / Mepitel)
Silver Sulfadiazine cream
🩺 Surgical diathermy preferred in children — less blood loss.
📍 Storage
ESCHAROTOMY EQUIPMENT ON BURNS TROLLEY
Post-Procedure
• Monitor CRT, Doppler pulse, SpO2 every 15 mins
• Re-dress with paraffin gauze / Mepitel
• Immediate transfer to Burns Centre if not already there
• Re-evaluate every 2-4 hours — may need repeat escharotomy
Anatomical Lines — Limb
1. Upper Limb: Medial and lateral incisions along the full length of the limb. Include thenar/hypothenar eminences and dorsal interossei if hand affected.
2. Lower Limb: Medial and lateral incisions. Include dorsum of foot if foot affected.

Critical: Do NOT extend into muscle/fascia (that is fasciotomy — requires surgical referral)
Anatomical Lines — Chest
1. Bilateral anterior axillary line incisions (vertical, clavicle to costal margin).
2. Connect across sternum with transverse subcostal incision if needed.
3. May also extend along costal margin bilaterally.

Incision depth = through full dermis to subcutaneous fat only.
Procedure Steps
  1. Analgesia + sedation — children have pain from surrounding partial thickness injury
  2. Clean area with chlorhexidine
  3. Mark incision lines with pen
  4. Scalpel incision through eschar to subcutaneous fat (not muscle)
  5. Check for adequate release: limb = palpable pulse returns; chest = improved chest rise
  6. Dress with paraffin gauze or Mepitel — do NOT use dry dressings
Escharotomy lines: medial/lateral limb + anterior axillary chest

Escharotomy (Burns)

Page 2: Action Flowchart — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

  • INDICATION: Full thickness circumferential burn + respiratory failure OR limb ischaemia | Contact Burns Centre EARLY
1
FLUID RESUSCITATION (FIRST)
  • Parkland: 3ml × kg × %TBSA (Hartmann's/Plasmalyte)
  • Half in 8hrs, half over 16hrs (from time of burn)
  • Target urine: >1ml/kg/hr (<30kg children)
🩺 Threshold ≥10% TBSA for resus in children (adults: 15%)
2
CHEST ESCHAROTOMY
  • Both anterior axillary line incisions — clavicle to costal margin
  • Transverse subcostal connection if needed
  • Depth: Dermis to fat only — NOT muscle
  • Check: Improved chest rise and ventilator pressures
3
LIMB ESCHAROTOMY — MEDIAL/LATERAL
  • Medial AND lateral incisions — full length of affected limb
  • Include hand/foot if involved
  • Depth: Dermis to fat — NOT into fascia
  • Check: Return of palpable pulse + Doppler signal
4
CONFIRM RELEASE & DRESS
  • Limb: Palpable pulse, CRT <2s, Doppler signal
  • Chest: Improved chest expansion, SpO2, reduced ventilator pressures
  • Dress with paraffin gauze / Mepitel
⚠️ If release inadequate
Fasciotomy required (surgical specialty). Do NOT attempt blind fasciotomy in ED.
Burns Centre contact mandatory. BBA referral: ≥2% TBSA in children. Do NOT delay transfer.

THORACIC EMERGENCY

Chest Drain (Open)

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
INDICATIONS: Tension pneumothorax (needle decompression first), Haemothorax, Massive haemothorax, Traumatic pneumothorax

• Needle decompression FIRST if tension: 2nd ICS mid-clavicular line OR 4th/5th ICS anterior axillary line
• Triangle of Safety: anterior border of latissimus dorsi, lateral border of pectoralis major, superior to nipple, below axilla
• Insert ABOVE the rib (avoid neurovascular bundle on inferior rib border)
• Ultrasound guidance strongly recommended where time allows

PAEDIATRIC SIZE GUIDE:
Chest drain (French) = 4 × uncuffed ETT size
<1 yr: 10-14 Fr | 1-5 yrs: 14-20 Fr
6-12 yrs: 20-24 Fr | >12 yrs: 24-28 Fr

NEVER use trochar technique in children
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Sterile Drape / Gown Pack
Scalpel: Size 10 or 15
Blunt dissection forceps (artery clip)
Chest drain — appropriately sized (see size guide)
Underwater seal drainage system
Large bore suction
Suture (1-0 Prolene / Silk)
Occlusive dressing
Lignocaine 1% (max 3mg/kg)
📍 Storage
CHEST DRAIN EQUIPMENT ON TRAUMA TROLLEY
Post-Procedure
• CXR to confirm position
• Monitor: If >10ml/kg in first hour → clamp and call thoracics
• Secure drain — never to clothing
• Suction: -5 to -10 cmH2O
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Position: Arm on affected side abducted and elevated above head.
  2. Identify: 4th or 5th ICS, anterior axillary line (Triangle of Safety). Mark with pen.
  3. Clean: Chlorhexidine. Drape. Infiltrate lignocaine — skin, rib, pleura (max 3mg/kg in children).
  4. Skin Incision: Horizontal incision parallel to rib, 1.5-2cm. Into subcutaneous fat.
  5. Blunt Dissection: Artery clip — dissect over superior border of rib into pleural space. Feel 'give' on entry.
  6. Finger Sweep: In older children (>8 yrs) — single finger sweep to confirm pleural space. In small children — NOT possible; use forceps to guide drain.
  7. Insert Drain: Feed drain WITHOUT trochar. Aim apex for air, base for fluid.
  8. Connect: Underwater seal. Confirm: Swinging + fogging of tube.
  9. Secure: Suture. Occlusive dressing. Mark drain at skin level.
🩺 No finger sweep in small children — insert with forceps guidance only. No trochar technique in any paediatric patient.
Triangle of Safety — 4th/5th ICS anterior axillary line

Chest Drain (Open)

Page 2: Action Flowchart — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

  • SIZE: 4×ETT size (French) | Triangle of Safety: 4th/5th ICS, anterior axillary line | NEVER use trochar in children
1
NEEDLE DECOMPRESSION (if tension suspected)
  • 2nd ICS mid-clavicular line OR 4th/5th ICS anterior axillary line
  • Use 14-16G cannula (18G if <5yrs)
  • Remove needle, leave cannula. Definitive drain follows.
🩺 In small children, needle decompression may need repeating — chest wall thinner and cannula kinks easily.
2
SKIN INCISION
  • Pause 5 seconds. Confirm triangle of safety landmarks.
  • Horizontal incision, 1.5-2cm, parallel to rib, above lower rib.
  • LA max: 3mg/kg lignocaine (NOT 5mg/kg — children more sensitive)
3
BLUNT DISSECTION & ENTRY
  • Artery clip over superior rib border into pleural space.
  • Feel 'give'. Do NOT use trochar.
  • Older children (>8): single finger sweep. Young children: forceps only.
4
INSERT DRAIN & CONNECT
  • Feed drain WITHOUT trochar. Aim: apex = air, base = fluid.
  • Connect underwater seal. Confirm swing/fogging.
  • Suture and dress. Mark at skin level.
If >10ml/kg drains in first hour — clamp and call thoracics immediately.
5
CONFIRM & ESCALATE
  • CXR to confirm position | Suction: -5 to -10 cmH2O | Monitor hourly drainage output
Massive haemothorax (>10ml/kg in first hour): CLAMP DRAIN. Activate Major Haemorrhage Protocol. Transfer to thoracics.

CARDIAC EMERGENCY

Pericardiocentesis

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
INDICATION: Cardiac tamponade causing haemodynamic compromise or cardiac arrest

Beck's Triad: Hypotension + Muffled heart sounds + JVD
(JVD unreliable in children — use USS)

• ULTRASOUND-GUIDED preferred at all times — blind technique only in arrest/immediately life-threatening
• USS findings: Pericardial effusion with right ventricular diastolic collapse
• In children: USS subxiphoid or parasternal view. Pericardium ~5cm from skin surface.
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Sterile Drape / Gown Pack
18G spinal needle or Seldinger pericardiocentesis kit
10/20ml Syringe with 3-way tap
Ultrasound machine (if available)
ECG monitoring (continuous)
Guidewire (if Seldinger technique)
Pigtail catheter (if indwelling drain)
Lignocaine 1% (max 3mg/kg)
📍 Storage
PERICARDIOCENTESIS KIT — CHECK LOCAL STORAGE LOCATION
Post-Procedure
• Leave drain/catheter in place if ongoing effusion likely
• Reassess ECG + USS
• ICU admission
• Monitor for recurrence (especially in malignancy, sepsis)
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Position: Supine, 30-45° head-up tilt (brings heart closer to chest wall).
  2. USS: Identify effusion. Mark optimal puncture site. Largest fluid pocket, shortest path. Mark on skin.
  3. Clean: Chlorhexidine. Sterile drape. LA at puncture site (3mg/kg max).
  4. Needle Insertion (Subxiphoid): 18G needle, subxiphoid angle — junction of xiphoid and left costal margin. Aim 15° posterior toward LEFT shoulder. Advance with continuous aspiration. USS-guided: watch needle tip enter pericardium.
  5. Aspiration: Aspirate pericardial fluid. Even 20-50ml can dramatically improve haemodynamics.
  6. Seldinger: If indwelling drain needed — introduce guidewire, remove needle, dilate, insert pigtail catheter.
  7. Secure: Suture. Connect to 3-way tap.

External (Transcutaneous) Pacing

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
INDICATIONS: Symptomatic bradycardia not responding to atropine/adrenaline | Complete heart block | Drug-induced bradycardia (beta-blocker, digoxin, CCB overdose)

CONTRAINDICATIONS: Hypothermia as primary cause (rewarm first) | Asystole in non-shockable cardiac arrest

PAEDS PACING SETTINGS:
Rate: 80-100 bpm (NOT adult default 60)
mA: Start at 10mA. Increase in 10mA increments until electrical capture
Output: Set 5-10mA ABOVE capture threshold
Mode: DEMAND if intrinsic rhythm present. NON-DEMAND during CPR or no intrinsic rhythm

PAD SIZE:
<15kg (approx <2yrs): Paediatric combo pads
>15kg: Adult combo pads acceptable
Placement: Anterior-posterior preferred (right of sternum + left posterior subscapular)
Equipment
Defibrillator/pacemaker unit (Zoll, Lifepak)
Paediatric combo pads (if <15kg)
Limb leads (4-lead ECG monitoring)
Backup IV/IO access
Analgesia + sedation (pacing is painful)
Post-Procedure
• Confirm mechanical capture: palpate femoral pulse for each paced beat
• Monitor BP
• Arrange urgent cardiology review
• Transfer to PICU/Cardiology
Procedure Steps
  1. MONITORING: Attach 4-lead ECG. Confirm bradycardia. Identify intrinsic rate and P-wave activity.
  2. ANALGESIA: If conscious — IV/IO analgesia NOW before pacing. Morphine 0.1mg/kg IV or Ketamine 0.5-1mg/kg IV
  3. PADS: Apply combo pads. <15kg = paediatric pads. Anterior-posterior placement preferred.
  4. SET RATE: 80-100 bpm
  5. SET mA TO ZERO: Confirm pacemaker recognising QRS complexes.
  6. INCREASE mA: Start at 10mA. Increase by 10mA increments. Electrical capture = pacing spike + wide QRS on ECG.
  7. SET FINAL OUTPUT: Set 5-10mA ABOVE capture threshold.
  8. CONFIRM MECHANICAL CAPTURE: Palpate pulse for each captured beat. DO NOT rely on ECG alone.
🩺 Pacing is painful. Do not delay analgesia in a conscious child. External pacing is a bridge — arrange definitive rhythm management urgently. 🩺 In CPR — use NON-DEMAND mode (chest compressions interfere with QRS sensing in demand mode).

Lateral Canthotomy & Cantholysis

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Indications
INDICATION: Orbital Compartment Syndrome (OCS) — retrobulbar haemorrhage causing:
• IOP >30-40 mmHg (normal <20 mmHg)
• Proptosis
• Relative afferent pupillary defect (RAPD)
• Reduced visual acuity
• Taut eyelids / chemosis

MECHANISM: Blunt orbital trauma (most common). Also: infection, burns, post-procedural.

TIME-CRITICAL: Vision loss within 60-100 minutes of ischaemia

🩺 Technique identical to adult. Procedural sedation MORE IMPORTANT in children — a moving patient risks globe injury.

ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION: Suspected globe rupture (irregular pupil, hyphema, laceration, low IOP <5mmHg)
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Topical anaesthetic eye drops (tetracaine/proxymetacaine)
LA: Lignocaine 1% with adrenaline — 2ml at lateral canthus
Straight haemostat (artery clip)
Small sterile scissors (iris scissors preferred)
Toothed forceps
Tonometer (Tono-Pen) if available
📍 Storage
CANTHOTOMY EQUIPMENT — CHECK LOCAL STORAGE LOCATION
Post-Procedure
• Re-measure IOP — should fall significantly
• If IOP still elevated: proceed to superior cantholysis
• Ophthalmology referral (urgent — same day or within hours)
• Wound heals spontaneously — no formal repair needed acutely
Procedure Steps
  1. ANALGESIA/SEDATION: Procedural sedation if child is not cooperative (vital — avoid globe injury). Ketamine 1-2mg/kg IV.
  2. TOPICAL ANAESTHESIA: Apply tetracaine drops to both eyes.
  3. LOCAL ANAESTHESIA: 2ml lignocaine 1% with adrenaline at lateral canthus. Inject parallel to globe surface — keep needle away from orbit.
  4. HAEMOSTAT CRUSH: Apply straight haemostat horizontally at lateral canthus for 30-60 seconds — devascularises tissue, reduces bleeding.
  5. CANTHOTOMY: Remove haemostat. Cut along crushed tissue from lateral canthus to lateral orbital rim (~1cm). Scissors aimed AWAY from globe (toward lateral orbital rim).
  6. IDENTIFY TENDON: Retract lower eyelid medially. Palpate/strum inferior crus of lateral canthal tendon with scissors tip.
  7. CANTHOLYSIS: Cut inferior canthal tendon — scissors pointed DOWN and AWAY from orbit. Lower lid should fall away.
  8. CHECK: Immediate globe proptosis and lower lid mobility confirm success.
🩺 Child cooperation is critical — do not perform this without sedation if child is moving. Ketamine procedural sedation is first-line.
Canthotomy then cantholysis: inferior crus first

Page 1: Walkthrough — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

Initial Actions & Recognition
RECOGNITION: Symptoms after local anaesthetic administration

CNS Features (early): Metallic taste, circumoral numbness, tinnitus, confusion, agitation
CNS Features (late): Seizures, coma, respiratory arrest

Ropivacaine: 3mg/kg
Prilocaine: 5mg/kg (alone) | 7mg/kg (with adrenaline)

⚠️ DIFFICULT AIRWAY TROLLEY
⚠️ DECLARE CICO — OPEN DIFFICULT AIRWAY TROLLEY — CALL 2222
AGE ≥ 8 YEARS: eFONA (Scalpel-Bougie-Tube)
AGE < 8 YEARS: SURGICAL TRACHEOTOMY — NOT CRICOTHYROTOMY
NEEDLES FAIL — DO NOT USE A NEEDLE
1
DECLARE & PREPARE
  • "Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate — proceeding to front of neck."
  • 100% O2. Neck extended. Open Difficult Airway Trolley.
  • AGE CHECK: ≥8 yrs → eFONA pathway | <8 yrs → Tracheotomy pathway
PATH A: eFONA (≥8 YEARS)
2a
INCISION (eFONA)
  • Pause 5 seconds. Laryngeal handshake — identify CTM.
  • Transverse STAB through CTM. Blade TOWARD YOU.
  • Rotate scalpel 90° (sharp edge toward feet). DO NOT WITHDRAW.
3a
RAILROAD (eFONA)
  • Insert bougie down FLAT SIDE of blade. 10-15cm. Feel clicks.
  • Remove scalpel. Railroad lubricated ETT = (Age÷4)+3.5 cuffed.
  • Twist to pass membrane. Inflate cuff.
PATH B: SURGICAL TRACHEOTOMY (<8 YEARS)
2b
INCISION (Tracheotomy)
  • Mark midline. Vertical skin incision — middle two quarters of neck length.
  • Blunt dissect through platysma to pre-tracheal fascia.
  • Bilateral retraction — maintain midline strictly.
3b
TRACHEAL ENTRY (Tracheotomy)
  • Identify tracheal rings. Cut 1-2 ANTERIOR rings only. Vertical incision.
  • Insert bougie through tracheal opening.
  • Railroad uncuffed ETT = (Age÷4)+4. Age-appropriate size.
4
VENTILATE & CONFIRM — BOTH PATHS
  • Connect BVM. CONFIRM EtCO2 (gold standard). Suction trachea.
  • Secure tube (tube tie — not tape alone). Call ENT — formal tracheostomy or repair in theatre.
⚠️ No EtCO2 after ETT placement
Likely oesophageal or paratracheal placement. WITHDRAW. Re-oxygenate with BVM face mask. Retry with fresh bougie. In small children: consider blind O2 via open tracheal incision (attach BVM to syringe barrel) while retrying.
After CICO event: ICU admission. ENT review for formal tracheostomy/repair. Debrief team. Document clearly in notes.

CARDIAC EMERGENCY

DC Cardioversion

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation) — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

🩺 PAEDIATRIC EDITION
Indications & Initial Actions
SYNCHRONISED (CARDIOVERSION):
• Haemodynamically unstable SVT (HR typically >220 bpm in infants, >180 bpm in children)
• Haemodynamically unstable AF/flutter
• Ventricular tachycardia with pulse

UNSYNCHRONISED (DEFIBRILLATION):
• VF or pulseless VT — PALS algorithm

Before cardioversion — try first if STABLE:
• Vagal manoeuvres: Ice to face <1yr (10 sec) | Valsalva >5yrs (blow through narrow straw) | One-handed carotid massage in older child
• Adenosine: 0.1 mg/kg IV rapid push + 10ml saline flush (max first dose 6mg). Repeat 0.2 mg/kg if no response (max 12mg). Use LARGE proximal vein.
• If adenosine fails OR haemodynamically unstable → DC cardioversion

Call 2222: Anaesthetics + Paediatric Reg. Sedation mandatory if conscious.
Paediatric Energy & Pad Guide
SYNCHRONISED CARDIOVERSION:
• First shock: 0.5–1 J/kg (biphasic)
• Second shock: 2 J/kg (biphasic)
• Maximum: 2 J/kg per shock
DEFIBRILLATION (VF/pVT — unsynchronised):
• 4 J/kg every shock — PALS algorithm
PAD SIZE:
• <10 kg (approx <1yr): Paediatric paddles/pads
• >10 kg: Adult pads acceptable

PAD PLACEMENT:
• Anterior-posterior preferred in small children (avoids pad overlap)
• Anterior-lateral acceptable if AP not feasible
Sedation for Cardioversion
Mandatory if patient is conscious.

First line: Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV slow push (dissociative, maintains airway)
Alternative: Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg IV + Fentanyl 1–2 mcg/kg IV

Have: BVM + suction + intubation equipment immediately available.
Monitoring: SpO2, EtCO2 (if intubated), ECG, NIBP throughout.
🩺 In haemodynamic collapse — do NOT delay cardioversion for sedation. Cardiovert immediately, then manage pain/distress.
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Vagal / Adenosine first if haemodynamically stable (see indications box).
  2. Monitoring: Continuous ECG, SpO2, NIBP. 12-lead ECG if time allows.
  3. IV/IO access: Large proximal vein. IO acceptable.
  4. Sedation: Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV if conscious. Give Now
  5. Pads: Apply combo pads — <10kg = paediatric; >10kg = adult. Anterior-posterior preferred.
  6. Sync Mode: CONFIRM SYNCHRONISED mode is ON (look for sync markers on QRS on ECG trace). Cardioversion only.
  7. Charge: 0.5–1 J/kg. Announce: "Charging — stand clear."
  8. Clear: Confirm all staff away. Oxygen away from patient face.
  9. Shock: Deliver. Assess rhythm immediately after.
  10. If no response: Increase to 2 J/kg. Repeat up to 3 times. Seek senior/cardiology input.
  11. Post-cardioversion: 12-lead ECG. IV amiodarone if recurrent VT: 5 mg/kg over 30 mins. Transfer to PICU.
Paediatric SVT — Quick Reference
Typical HR: Infants >220 bpm | Children >180 bpm
ECG: Narrow complex tachycardia, no P-waves (or retrograde P), abrupt onset/offset

Stable SVT pathway:
1. Vagal manoeuvre (ice face / Valsalva)
2. Adenosine 0.1 mg/kg IV rapid push (max 6mg)
3. Adenosine 0.2 mg/kg if no response (max 12mg)
4. Synchronised DC cardioversion 0.5–1 J/kg if above fail

Unstable SVT (shock/collapse):
→ Synchronised DC cardioversion immediately 0.5–1 J/kg
Do NOT give adenosine to haemodynamically compromised patients — may precipitate cardiac arrest.
📍 EQUIPMENT LOCATION
DEFIBRILLATOR IN RESUS BAY. PAEDIATRIC PADS IN PAEDIATRIC RESUS.

DC Cardioversion

Page 2: Action Flowchart — PAEDIATRIC (<16 YRS)

🩺 PAEDIATRIC EDITION
  • SYNC ON for all cardioversion (non-arrest). SYNC OFF for defibrillation (VF/pVT).
  • Pads: <10 kg = paediatric pads. >10 kg = adult pads acceptable.
  • Sedate before shocking if conscious — Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV.
1
STABLE SVT — TRY FIRST
  • Vagal manoeuvres: Ice to face <1yr (10 sec) | Valsalva >5yrs (blow through straw)
  • Adenosine: 0.1 mg/kg IV rapid push + 10ml saline flush (max 6mg first dose). Large proximal vein. Give Fast
  • Repeat: 0.2 mg/kg if no response (max 12mg).
  • Do NOT give adenosine if haemodynamically compromised — go straight to cardioversion.
2
SEDATE & PREPARE
  • Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV slow push (if conscious). Give Now
  • Apply combo pads: <10kg = paediatric; >10kg = adult. Anterior-posterior preferred.
  • CONFIRM SYNC MODE ON — look for sync markers on QRS complexes on ECG display.
3
SHOCK — SYNCHRONISED CARDIOVERSION
  • Set energy: 0.5–1 J/kg (biphasic).
  • Charge. Announce: "Stand clear — oxygen away."
  • Confirm all clear. Deliver shock.
  • Assess rhythm immediately. If no response → 2 J/kg. Repeat up to 3 attempts.
  • 🩺 After each shock — reconfirm SYNC mode is still ON (some monitors reset to async after shock).
4
DEFIBRILLATION — VF / pVT (UNSYNCHRONISED)
  • Confirm SYNC MODE OFF. VF/pVT = unsynchronised shock.
  • Energy: 4 J/kg every shock — PALS algorithm.
  • Immediately resume CPR after shock. 2 min cycle before rhythm check.
  • Same energy for all subsequent defibrillation shocks: 4 J/kg.
5
POST-CARDIOVERSION
  • 12-lead ECG immediately.
  • Recurrent/refractory VT: Amiodarone 5 mg/kg IV over 30 mins. Fetch Drug
  • Recurrent SVT: Discuss with Paediatric Cardiology — consider adenosine repeat or digoxin.
  • Transfer to PICU. Continuous cardiac monitoring.
⚠️ No response after 3 shocks
Call Paediatric Cardiology urgently. Consider: correct paddle position, adequate contact, sync mode status, underlying reversible cause (electrolytes, hypoxia). Magnesium 25–50 mg/kg IV (max 2g) for torsades de pointes.

CARDIAC EMERGENCY

STOP 5 Hot Debrief

Perform immediately after the event, before the team disperses. 5 minutes maximum.

1. Summarise the case (1 minute).
2. Things that went well?
3. Opportunities to improve?
4. Points for action (Fix equipment, report incidents).
5. 5 minutes max — then check on each other.
🩺 Paediatric-Specific Debrief Questions:
• Was age-appropriate equipment used (ETT size, pacing pads, drain size)?
• Was weight estimated correctly (Broselow tape / formula)?
• Were age-appropriate drug doses used (weight-based)?
• Was IO access available and used appropriately?
• Was the correct surgical airway technique used for the patient's age (eFONA ≥8 yrs / Tracheotomy <8 yrs)?
• Was the Paediatric Registrar involved?
• Was DATIX / incident report completed?
Is everyone okay? Does anyone need support right now?
Check on your colleagues — high acuity paediatric events are extremely distressing.

Escalate to Consultant within 24 hours. Complete team debrief within 48 hours.
